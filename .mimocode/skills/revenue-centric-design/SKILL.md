# Revenue-Centric Design Skill

> A distilled playbook for designing SaaS & startup products that **convert, retain, and monetize**.

## When to Use

- Designing landing pages or marketing sites
- Building pricing pages
- Creating onboarding flows
- Working on conversion optimization
- Building any user-facing product interface
- Reviewing product design decisions

## The RCD Spine: 9 Core Principles

1. **Neutrality is omission** — an interface that doesn't direct hurts conversion.
2. **Who talks to everyone convinces no one** — no ICP → generic value → worse retention.
3. **Value first, ask later** — proof must arrive before the user questions their choice.
4. **Your promise is the size of your proof** — the market believes what you demonstrate, not what you claim.
5. **Same competes on price, different on category** — no contrast, no margin.
6. **Default is the decision you made for the user** — the initial state defines mass behavior.
7. **Retention is built, not requested** — perceived loss retains more than promised benefit.
8. **Expansion is born of usage** — upgrade at the moment of the limit, never by interruption.
9. **Price is a filter** — pricing defines who enters, who stays, and who expands.

## Implementation Checklist

When designing any product interface:

- [ ] **ICP Clarity**: Who is this for? What do they value?
- [ ] **Value Proposition**: Is the promise clear and demonstrable?
- [ ] **Default State**: What decision have we made for the user?
- [ ] **Social Proof**: Is proof visible before asking for commitment?
- [ ] **Pricing**: Does it use anchoring/decoy effects?
- [ ] **Retention**: Are we building loss aversion?
- [ ] **Expansion**: Are upgrades triggered by usage limits?
- [ ] **Conversion**: Is there a clear, single CTA?

## Review Questions

1. Does this interface direct the user toward a specific action?
2. Is the value proposition tailored to a specific ICP?
3. Does proof come before the ask?
4. Are defaults set to guide behavior?
5. Is pricing designed to anchor and filter?
6. Are retention mechanisms built into the experience?
7. Is expansion triggered by usage, not interruption?
